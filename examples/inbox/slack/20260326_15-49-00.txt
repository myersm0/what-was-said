Are there any pitfalls with energy conservation?

A. Gupta
:gpu:
Sunday at 6:42 PM
Big one: if your specular lobe doesn't conserve energy, your metals will darken. Always make sure the integral of your BRDF over the hemisphere is ≤ 1. Disney's model handles this by tying roughness to the specular lobe shape.

sarah_c
Sunday at 6:45 PM
We also recommend reading Brent Burley's follow-up work on skin shading. It covers subsurface scattering which isn't in the original Disney paper but is useful for organic materials.
